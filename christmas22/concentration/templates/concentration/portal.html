Christmas Concentration Game

Well, I think I've got it working after a fashion. Not perfectly, but better than nothing. This is really a proof of concept type of thing to see if I can write regular computer games for use on the web.

To play, click one of the numbered squares to reveal the "prize" underneath. Click another square to try to match prizes. If the prizes don't match, click again and the board returns to its former state. If they do match, several things happen. The prize appears on your list at the left, its value is added to your score and, when you click again, two pieces of the puzzle are revealed. You get ten seconds to decide if you can solve it. If so, click the "Solve" button and enter your solution in the box that appears at the bottom. If you are right the current Puzzle Bonus is added to your score and the game is over. If not, click again and the board returns to its former state. As you play the game, the status area at the bottom will remind you of what to do next.

Each puzzle will combine pictures, letters, and words to spell out something familiar. For instance, the puzzle at the right starts with the sound of the letter "b", adds the highlighted East indicator on a weather vane, spells out the word "of", shows a bird and adds the sound of the letter "N". So, we get b + East of bird + n, or Beast of Burden. Quotes around a letter means the name of the letter, as is often done in texting. Thus "R" can represent the word "are." When checking solutions the program isn't fussy about UPPER CASE or lower case, or extra spaces between the words. It is VERY fussy about spelling and punctuation however. If a word has an apostrophe, you've got to have that apostrophe in your solution. All of the puzzles in Christmas Concentration will be related to Christmas, of course.

There are some special "prize" cards to know about:

  1. There are two WILD CARDs in every game. A wild card matches anything, including another wild card. Most of the time the wild cards in a game will end up matching different prizes, so there will be two uncovered squares at the end of the game.

  2. There are four FORFEIT ONE GIFT cards in every game. Matching them means that you have to select one of the gifts in your list, which will be highlighted in red, to give up. The points for that prize will be subtracted from your score. If you don't yet have any prizes, nothing happens -- lucky you!

  3. There are four TAKE ONE GIFT cards in every game. Matching them means that you get to select a gift from a set of gifts that do not appear in this game. A window will appear with a list of five items to choose from. Select the one you think will be worth the most points.

  4. There are two SNEAK PEEK cards in every game. Matching them means that after the two puzzle pieces are revealed, and you get a chance to solve the puzzle, you will get to select another covered square to briefly take a look at the puzzle piece underneath it. If you think you know the puzzle before peeking, though, go ahead and guess it. The Puzzle Bonus decreases a little after a Peek.
